## The post (as it would publish)

> 7 days. 4 weeks. 6 weeks. 6 months.
>
> That's how long it takes to read a real signal from paid, founder content, cold outbound, and SEO. In that order.
>
> A founder asked me last week which channel she should pick. She had $8K to spend and a board meeting in 6 weeks. The right answer wasn't the channel with the highest theoretical ceiling. It was the channel that could tell her *why* it was failing inside her timeline.
>
> Most early-stage growth experiments don't work the first time. The channel you pick is really a bet on how fast you can debug the thing that didn't work.
>
> SEO needs 6 months to read the signal. She didn't have 6 months. She didn't even have 6 weeks of patience.
>
> Next time you're picking a channel, ask one question first: how fast can I see why this is failing?

---

## First comment

> One nuance I left out: this only holds at the pre-Series A stage. Once you're past $1M ARR and have a brand that ranks for anything, SEO's debug loop changes — you're optimizing existing pages, not waiting for new ones to index. At that stage SEO is fast. Just not when you're starting from zero.
