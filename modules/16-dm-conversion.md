# DM Conversion Playbook

Content generates visibility. DMs convert visibility into business. Without this module, the workflow is a content tool, not a growth engine.

## When Someone DMs After a Post

**Type 1: Compliment DM** ("Great post!" / "Really resonated")
- Reply: Thank them briefly, ask what specifically resonated. This surfaces their actual problem.
- Example: "Thanks — what part hit closest to home for you?"
- Do NOT pitch. Do NOT send a link. Let them talk.

**Type 2: Question DM** ("How did you handle X?" / "Can you explain Y?")
- Reply: Answer the question genuinely in 2-3 sentences. Then ask about their context.
- Example: Answer, then "Are you running into this with your own product?"
- If they confirm a problem → move to Type 3.

**Type 3: Lead Inquiry DM** ("Do you offer consulting?" / "Could you help with X?")
- Reply: Acknowledge, ask 2 qualifying questions, suggest a call.
- Example: "I do — what are you working on right now, and what's the timeline looking like? Might be worth a quick call to see if there's a fit."
- Never send pricing in DM. Always move to call.

## Lead Qualification from Comment Behavior

Before DMing someone who engaged with your post, check:
- Did they comment substantively (not just "Great post")?
- Does their headline/role match your ICP?
- Have they engaged with multiple posts (warm lead)?

**Warm lead signals (engage proactively):**
- Commented on 2+ posts in 30 days
- Asked a specific question in comments
- Headline matches ICP keywords

**Cold signal (don't DM):**
- Only reacted (no comment)
- Headline doesn't match ICP
- First-time engagement, generic comment

## DM Etiquette Rules (Shared — also applies to Module 17 follow-ups)

These rules govern ALL DMs: post-engagement, connection follow-ups, and lead conversations.

- Never pitch in the first DM. Start a conversation.
- Max 1 DM per lead per week
- Reference their specific comment or post engagement: "Saw your comment on my post about X — you mentioned Y, curious about that"
- Keep DMs under 50 words. Long DMs don't get read.
- Wait 24-48 hours after connection acceptance before DMing (looks automated otherwise)

**Cross-reference:** Module 17 (Connection Growth) references these rules for connection follow-ups.

## From DM to Call
The goal is always to move from DM → call within 2-3 exchanges:
1. They reach out or you reach out (referencing their engagement)
2. They describe their situation (you ask one clarifying question)
3. You suggest a call ("Sounds like there might be something worth exploring. Want to do a quick 15-min call this week?")

Never sell in DMs. DMs qualify. Calls convert.
