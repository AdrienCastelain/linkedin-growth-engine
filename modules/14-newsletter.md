# LinkedIn Newsletter Strategy

LinkedIn newsletters are the highest-reach content type on the platform since late 2025. A newsletter post reaches 100% of subscribers by email notification, bypassing the algorithm entirely.

## When to Launch a Newsletter

**Launch criteria:**
- You have 500+ followers (LinkedIn's minimum for newsletter access)
- Your goal is "thought leadership," "grow audience," or "inbound leads"
- You can commit to a consistent publishing cadence (biweekly minimum)
- You have enough depth in your expertise to sustain long-form content

**Don't launch if:**
- You can't maintain cadence (missed newsletters damage subscriber trust)
- Your goal is employer brand or community growth (newsletters are less relevant)
- Your content pillars don't support long-form exploration

## Newsletter Structure

**Frequency:** Biweekly (every 2 weeks) is the sweet spot. Weekly is ambitious. Monthly loses momentum.

**Length:** 800-1,500 words. Longer than a post, shorter than a blog article. Must reward the time investment.

**Format:**
1. Hook paragraph (same "see more" principles as posts — the email subject line IS the hook)
2. Core insight or framework (the main value)
3. Practical application (how the reader uses this)
4. Closing that connects to next issue or invites response

## Cross-Promotion Rules

**From posts to newsletter:**
- 1 post per month should directly reference the newsletter ("I wrote about this in more depth in my newsletter — link in comments")
- First comment on that post contains the newsletter subscribe link
- Never make every post a newsletter promo — 1 per month max

**From newsletter to posts:**
- Each newsletter can generate 2-3 post ideas (pull out key insights as standalone posts)
- Post the insight, then reference the newsletter for the full version
- This creates a content flywheel: posts drive subscribers, newsletter rewards them with depth

## Newsletter vs Posts — Content Split

| Content type | Posts | Newsletter |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| Quick insights, opinions, stories | ✓ | |
| Frameworks and deep analysis | Summary post → | Full version here |
| Industry data with interpretation | Headline + take | Full data + methodology |
| Case studies | Brief version | Detailed with learnings |
| Personal stories | ✓ | Expanded with reflection |

## Subscriber Growth Tactics
- Feature box in LinkedIn profile (automatic when newsletter is created)
- Cross-promote in first comment once per month
- Reference newsletter content in comments on others' posts (when genuinely relevant)
- Include newsletter link in connection request follow-up DM (after they accept, not in the request)

## Newsletter Content Repurposing
Each newsletter issue should produce:
- 2-3 standalone post ideas (pulled from key insights)
- 1 poll idea (testing a hypothesis from the newsletter)
- Social proof for expertise pillar posts ("In my latest newsletter, I broke down...")
