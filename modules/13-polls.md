# Poll Strategy

Polls generate +206% reach compared to text posts. At least 1 poll per month is mandatory.

## Structure Rules
- **2-4 options** (not 1, never more than 4). 2-option polls create the clearest debates. 4-option polls get higher participation but less discussion.
- **Poll duration:** 1 week (default). 24 hours only for timely/trending topics.
- **Options must be genuinely distinct.** If two options are variations of the same answer, collapse them.
- **Include a "surprising" option** that makes voters think. Polls where the outcome is obvious (90/10 split) generate votes but no comments.

## The Follow-Up Post Pattern (Critical)
The follow-up analysis post consistently outperforms the poll itself. Schedule it 24 hours after the poll closes.

**Follow-up structure:**
1. Share the results (screenshot or text summary)
2. Name the winning option and what it reveals about the audience
3. Add your interpretation — what surprised you, what confirmed your hypothesis
4. Close with a question about why the results went that way

**Example:**
```
Poll: "What kills more SaaS products: bad UX or bad pricing?"
Results: Bad UX 62%, Bad pricing 38%

Follow-up post:
"I asked last week: what kills more SaaS products — bad UX or bad pricing?
62% said bad UX.
I was in the minority. Here's why I think pricing kills more quietly..."
[your take, 200-300 words, closing question]
```

## Poll Topic Selection
- Stay within content pillars
- Best poll types for engagement:
  - "Which matters more" (binary choice with genuine tension)
  - "What would you do" (scenario-based, readers project their experience)
  - "What's your biggest challenge with X" (ICP self-identification — gold for inbound leads goal)
- Avoid: trivia, "what's your favorite X" (low value, high participation, no conversion)

## Goal-Specific Poll Guidance

| Goal | Poll strategy |
|------|--------------|
| Inbound leads | "What's your biggest challenge with X" — commenters self-identify as ICP |
| Thought leadership | "Which matters more: X or Y" — drives debate in comments |
| Grow audience | Any poll — raw participation drives reach |
| Employer brand | "What do you value most in a workplace" — candidates self-identify |
