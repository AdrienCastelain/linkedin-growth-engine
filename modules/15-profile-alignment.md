# Profile Alignment

The LinkedIn profile is the conversion surface. Brilliant content with a weak profile means visitors arrive and leave. Profile elements must align with content pillars and posting strategy.

## Headline
- Must reflect your positioning, not your job title
- Should be immediately recognizable to your ICP
- Updated when content strategy changes
- Test: if someone reads your last 5 posts and then sees your headline, does it click?

**Good:** "I redesign complex products so founders stop losing users at step 3"
**Bad:** "Product Designer | UX | Strategy"

## About Section
- First 3 lines visible without expanding — same "see more" engineering as posts
- Must address the ICP's problem directly, not list credentials
- Include a clear call to action (book a conversation, visit site, subscribe to newsletter)
- Voice must match post voice (if posts are casual and direct, About can't be formal and corporate)

## Featured Section
- Pin your 3-5 best-performing posts (rotate monthly)
- Include newsletter link if applicable
- Include website or booking link
- Order: most relevant to ICP first, not most recent

## Experience Section
- Current role should frame the value proposition, not list responsibilities
- Use the same language as your content pillars
- Include specific outcomes with numbers where possible

## Skills Section
- Top 3 visible skills should be strategic, not tactical
- "Product Strategy" over "Wireframing"
- "Design Leadership" over "Figma"
- Reorder to match positioning when content strategy changes

## Profile-Content Coherence Check (Monthly)
Run this check alongside the monthly performance review:
1. Read your last 10 posts. What do they say you're about?
2. Read your headline and About. Does it match?
3. Check Featured section. Are the pinned posts still your best performers?
4. If any element is misaligned, update it.

## Goal-Specific Profile Optimization

| Goal | Priority profile element |
|------|------------------------|
| Inbound leads | Headline (ICP problem), About (clear CTA), Featured (case studies) |
| Thought leadership | About (point of view), Featured (best thought pieces), Newsletter link |
| Grow audience | Headline (curiosity-driving), Featured (most-shared posts) |
| Employer brand | About (culture and values), Experience (team achievements) |
| Speaking/media | Featured (talk recordings, media mentions), About (speaking topics) |
