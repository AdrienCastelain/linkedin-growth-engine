# Content Decay & Evergreen Recycling

4 posts/week should not require 4 entirely new ideas every week. High-performing content has a second life.

## Identifying Evergreen Content

After 30 days, tag each post as:
- **Evergreen** — valuable regardless of when posted (frameworks, lessons, opinions)
- **Timely** — tied to a specific event or trend (expires)
- **Story** — personal narrative (can be re-angled but not reposted)

## Recycling Rules

### Repost with Fresh Angle (90-day minimum gap)
Take a high-performing evergreen post and rewrite it:
- Different hook (same insight, new entry point)
- Different closing (question instead of nudge, or vice versa)
- Updated with new data or a fresh example
- Must pass all quality gates as if it were new

**Example:**
```
Original (January): "Most redesigns fail because nobody asked why users leave."
Recycled (April): "A client asked me to redesign their checkout. I asked them why users leave instead. Different question, different outcome."
Same insight, completely different post.
```

### Format Conversion
Turn a high-performing text post into a different format:
- Text post → carousel (expand each paragraph into a slide)
- Text post → poll (extract the core tension as a poll question)
- Post + comment thread → follow-up post (synthesize the discussion)
- Long post → short punchy version (same insight, half the words)

### "Greatest Hits" Rotation
- Keep a running list of the top 5 performing posts per quarter
- Feature them in the profile's Featured section (rotate monthly)
- Reference them in new content: "I wrote about this a few months ago and the response surprised me..."

## Story Bank Management

Stories are the most valuable raw material. Without management, the same 5 stories recycle until they're stale.

**Collection protocol:**
- After every notable interaction, note potential stories (2-3 sentences)
- Tag by pillar (expertise, personal, engagement, promotional)
- Tag by theme (failure, success, surprise, lesson)
- Include specific details: names (with permission), numbers, timeframes

**Freshness rules:**
- A story used in a post enters a 90-day cooldown
- A story used in 3 posts enters a 180-day cooldown
- Stories never fully expire — they can be re-angled indefinitely with fresh framing

**When the story bank is empty:**
- Default to opinion/framework posts (don't require stories)
- Reduce Personal story pillar weight temporarily (from 30% to 15%)
- Prompt yourself: "Running low on fresh stories. Any recent wins, lessons, or surprises?"
- Pull from other channels: podcast appearances, conference talks, blog posts, email exchanges
