# Cultural Market Profiles

LinkedIn best practices are implicitly American. This module provides market-specific overrides that load alongside your brand profile.

## US (Default)
- Direct, bold, high-frequency posting
- Self-promotion is expected and rewarded
- Contrarian takes perform well
- 4 posts/week is standard
- "I built this" / "Here's what I learned" are standard hooks

## UK / EU
- Slightly more reserved tone than US
- Lower promotional ratio (max 1 promotional post per month, not 2)
- Understatement outperforms assertion ("This surprised me" > "This changed everything")
- Humor travels differently — dry wit works, enthusiasm can read as American
- 3-4 posts/week

## Australia / New Zealand (ANZ)
- **Tall poppy syndrome is real.** Posts that read as self-aggrandizing ("Here's what 17 years taught me," "I built a framework that...") can trigger negative reactions. Not always, but the risk is higher than in the US.
- **Collective framing outperforms individual framing.** "What I've seen in teams" > "What I achieved." "We discovered" > "I discovered."
- **Question-led > declaration-led.** Start with curiosity, not authority. "Has anyone else noticed..." outperforms "Here's the truth about..."
- **3 posts/week recommended default** for sub-5K accounts. Posting more frequently risks overexposure in a smaller professional network.
- **Humor and self-deprecation land well.** "Honestly, I got this wrong three times before it clicked" plays better than in the US.
- **Local hashtags matter.** #NZBusiness, #NZTech have tiny audiences but high engagement rates.
- **Relationship signals matter more.** Commenters expect a reply. Ignoring comments in a small market is noticed.

## APAC (General — Singapore, HK, Japan, Korea)
- Face-saving conscious — avoid content that could embarrass the reader's company or industry
- Formality calibration varies by country: Singapore is more direct, Japan is more formal
- Collective framing (see ANZ) but even stronger — "we" over "I"
- Lower posting frequency (2-3/week) in markets where LinkedIn is secondary to local platforms. **Cultural override supersedes global minimum (3/week) for markets where LinkedIn is secondary.**
- Professional credentials carry more weight — mention certifications, institutional affiliations

## How to Apply

1. During onboarding, identify your **primary audience market** (not where you are based, but where your ICP lives)
2. Load the appropriate cultural profile
3. Apply as overrides to the default (US) rules
4. If your audience is multi-market (e.g., APAC + US), use the more conservative profile as the default and flag when a post might land differently across markets

## Tall Poppy Test (ANZ-Specific — Run on Every Post)

These questions catch content that would land fine in the US but trigger negative reactions in ANZ:

1. **"Would a Kiwi/Aussie mate roll their eyes at this?"** — If the hook sounds like a TED talk intro ("Here's what 15 years taught me about leadership"), reframe as observation or question.
2. **"Am I claiming credit or sharing a pattern?"** — "I built a framework that..." → "A pattern I keep seeing in teams..." Same insight, different framing.
3. **"Would I say this at a BBQ without sounding like a wanker?"** — The BBQ test is the ANZ equivalent of the Colleague Test. If the answer is no, tone it down.

## Cultural Override Checklist (Run on Every Post for Non-US Markets)

- [ ] Would this post read as boastful in the target market?
- [ ] Does the self-promotion level match market norms?
- [ ] Is the framing collective ("teams," "we") or individual ("I")?
- [ ] Would the humor/tone translate?
- [ ] Are local hashtags included?
- [ ] (ANZ) Does this post pass all 3 Tall Poppy Test questions above?
